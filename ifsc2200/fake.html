Social Media and its ethical concerns

Social media has become an integral part of our lives, with millions of people using platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram to connect with friends, share information, and express themselves. However, the rise of social media has also brought unethical issues with it such as an increase in cyberbullying, as well as a breeding ground for fake news.

Fake News

Social media has revolutionized the way we access and consume news. However, it has also created new ethical challenges, particularly around the spread of fake news. It can have serious consequences, including undermining public trust in the media, promoting harmful behaviors, and influencing political outcomes.

The spread of fake news has significant implications for democracy. It has already created a lot of distrust amongst the younger generations against quite a few news outlets, media, journalists. It can also distort public opinion. Fake news can also be used to manipulate political outcomes, as demonstrated by the 2016 US Presidential election. In that election, Russian operatives used social media to disseminate fake news stories to influence the election outcome. This raises ethical concerns around the use of social media to interfere with democratic processes and highlights the importance of addressing the issue of fake news.

Social media platforms have a responsibility to ensure that their platforms are not used to spread fake news. They have been criticized for not doing enough to prevent the spread of fake news and for not taking action against those who spread it.

However, one challenge for this is the volume of information that is present on the platforms. It can be difficult to identify and remove fake news stories, particularly when they are shared by multiple users. Although some people might disagree with social media platforms banning certain stuff, it is necessary to prevent situation such as the ones in the articles below. Additionally, social media platforms have been criticized for prioritizing engagement over accuracy, as the algorithms that determine what content is shown to users are designed to maximize user engagement, rather than accuracy.

Individuals also have a responsibility to address the issue of fake news. As consumers of news, people must be vigilant in checking the accuracy of the stories they read and share. This includes checking the sources of the information and verifying the claims made in the story. They also have a responsibility to report fake news stories to social media platforms when they encounter them. People can also engage in online discussions around the issue of fake news, promoting critical thinking and media literacy.

In conclusion, fake news is a significant ethical issue associated with social media. It has serious implications for democracy, public trust in the media, and the spread of accurate information. This includes investing in resources to identify and remove fake news stories and promoting accuracy over engagement. Lawmakers also have a role to play, particularly in regulating the use of social media to interfere with democratic processes. By working together, we can address the issue of fake news and ensure that social media is used as a tool for promoting accurate information and informed public discourse.

Fake News and Shooting

Pizzeria Attack, Fake News Brought Real Guns

  • On Sunday, December 4, 2016, a shooting incident occurred at a pizza shop in northwestern Washington D.C. during the middle of the day. This was in a busy but peaceful shopping district, and the shop was filled with families. A man brought a rifle into the shop and began shooting. Fortunately, no one was hurt, and the suspect was arrested
  • The pizza shop, called Comet Ping Pong, had become embroiled in a strange situation due to an event that had occurred about one month earlier. There were false tweets widely spread on the net claiming that this pizza shop was the base for a pedophile sex ring involving Democratic presidential candidate Hillary Clinton, a former Secretary of State, and members of her campaign. The operators of the pizza shop began receiving threats from right wing activists who believed that the reports were true.
  • The anonymous bulletin board sites then focused their attention on the pizza shop called Comet Ping Pong, which was frequently mentioned in the e-mail of John Podesta, head of the Clinton campaign, whose e-mails were being successively leaked on the whistle-blower site WikiLeaks at about this same time. This escalated into posts that this shop was the site of child sex trafficking. The day before the voting in the presidential election, the hashtag “#pizzagate” appeared.
  • Although social media subsequently banned posts related to pizzagate, the threats did not stop, culminating in the appearance of a 28-year-old man from North Carolina, who showed up at the shop with a rifle to do his own “investigation.” According to a New York Times interview with the suspect after his capture, he was a soft-spoken, polite man who intended to rescue the children trapped in the shop.

Covid Fake News

Reports regarding misinformation and how it affects people

  • Four studies reviewed in the paper looked at the proportion of health misinformation on social media, and found that it reached up to 51% in posts associated with vaccines, up to 28.8% in posts associated with COVID-19, and up to 60% in posts related to pandemics. Among YouTube videos about emerging infectious diseases, 20–30% were found to contain inaccurate or misleading information.
  • The systematic review found that people feel mental, social, political and/or economic distress due to misleading and false health-related content on social media during pandemics, health emergencies and humanitarian crises.
  • Experts and health professionals are among those best placed to refute misinformation and direct users to information sources that are evidence-based. Countermeasures include awareness campaigns for patients and health-care professionals, platforms with evidence-based data, the inclusion of scientific evidence in health-related content in mass media, and efforts to improve media and health literacy.
